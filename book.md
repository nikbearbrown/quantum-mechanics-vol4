# Quantum Mechanics Vol. 4: Information, Measurement, and Open Systems

**Author:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## One-Sentence Pitch
The simulation-first undergraduate volume that teaches the student to use the density matrix, reason about entanglement and Bell tests, run basic quantum-information protocols, and read current quantum-hardware literature.

## The Argument
Quantum mechanics is hard because its objects have no classical analogue and the standard textbook gives no way to build intuition for them. This volume pairs every concept with an LLM-generated, student-modifiable D3 simulation, so the student sees each new object before manipulating its algebra. It is Volume 4 of the *Quantum Mechanics* series.

## The Gap
Griffiths/Townsend are rigorous but static; video and applets are visual but non-rigorous and unmodifiable. This volume gives the student a third object: a simulation they built, governed by physics they can derive, that they can change and watch respond.

## The Reader
Senior elective / quantum-information bridge. Enters with the prerequisites carried from the prior volume (or, for Volume 1, ODEs, complex numbers, and introductory mechanics/waves). Wants to *do* quantum mechanics, not just read about it. The optional **Quantum Mechanics Vol. 5 — Math for Quantum Physics** companion supplies any math refresher, indexed to the chapter the student is on.

## High-Level Outline
- Act One (1–3): the modern state — density matrix, composite systems, Bell.
- Act Two (4–6): information and openness — gates, teleportation, decoherence.
- Act Three (7–10): measurement, hardware, error, and research literacy.
